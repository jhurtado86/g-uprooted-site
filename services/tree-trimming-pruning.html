What Tree Trimming & Pruning Involves — and When Your Trees Need It

Trimming and pruning is the regular care that keeps a tree healthy instead of hazardous. We cut away the dead, broken, and diseased limbs, open up a dense canopy so wind and light can pass through, and shape the tree so it grows strong in the right direction. Done right, it's the difference between a tree that handles a Rio Grande Valley storm and one that loses a heavy branch onto your roof.

Our crew prunes with a plan. We look at the whole tree, find the limbs that are dead, crossing, or hanging too close to a roof, fence, or power line, and make clean cuts in the right spots so the tree heals well. Then we rake up and haul off every branch, so the only thing you notice is a tidier, safer yard. We do this for homes and businesses across Harlingen, McAllen, Brownsville, and the rest of the RGV all year long.

We also know when to stop. We don't top trees or strip them bare — that hurts the tree and weakens it for years. We trim what needs trimming, tell you straight what your tree actually needs, and give you an honest, upfront price first. The estimate is always free.

Questions About Tree Trimming

How often should I trim my trees?
Most trees in the Rio Grande Valley do well with a trim every one to three years, depending on the type of tree and how fast it grows. Young trees benefit from light, regular pruning to set a good shape. Big shade trees usually need a thinning every couple of years. Call us and we'll tell you what your specific trees need.
When is the best time to trim trees in the RGV?
The cooler months are usually best, when the tree is less stressed and you can trim before hurricane season. Oaks are the exception — pruning them in late winter and spring can spread oak wilt, so we plan those cuts for a safer time and seal them. That said, dead or dangerous limbs can come off any time of year. Call us and we'll work out the right timing for your trees.
Will trimming hurt my tree?
Not when it's done right. Good pruning makes a tree healthier — it takes weight off weak limbs and lets in light and air. The harm comes from topping or over-cutting, which we don't do. We make clean cuts in the right spots and never take more than the tree can handle, so it heals and keeps growing strong.
Do you trim palm trees too?
We do — palms are one of our specialties. Trimming dead fronds, skinning, and shaping palms takes a different touch than shade trees. See our palm tree trimming and removal page, or just call and tell us what you've got.
Can trimming help before hurricane season?
Yes — it's one of the smartest things you can do for a tree in the Valley. Thinning a heavy canopy lets storm wind pass through instead of pushing on the whole tree, and clearing out dead limbs takes away the branches most likely to break and fly. If a storm does take a tree down, we also offer 24/7 storm damage and emergency service.
Do you clean up and haul away the branches?
Always. Cleanup is part of every trimming job — we rake up the branches and leaves and haul it all off. Your yard is clean when we leave, with no pile left behind for you to deal with.
