When a Tree Comes Down, Every Hour Counts

A storm-damaged tree is a different kind of problem than a tree you've been meaning to deal with. A trunk split over the bedroom, a limb pinning a car, a whole tree across the only way out of the driveway — these are dangerous, and they get worse the longer they sit. That's why we run emergency tree service around the clock. You call, we answer, and we get a crew moving.

The Rio Grande Valley sees its share of rough weather — hurricane season, tropical storms, and the hard straight-line winds that come with them. After a big blow, trees that looked fine can be cracked, uprooted, or hung up and ready to drop. We respond across Harlingen, McAllen, Brownsville, and the rest of the RGV, day or night, to take the danger down safely before anyone gets hurt or the damage spreads.

Our first job on any emergency call is to make the area safe — then clear the tree, haul off the debris, and leave you with a property you can actually use again. We'll document the damage with photos for your insurance claim if you need it, and every estimate is free, even in the middle of a storm cleanup.

Questions About Emergency Tree Service

Are you really available 24/7?
Yes. Tree emergencies don't keep business hours, so we don't either. Call 956-536-2384 any time — day, night, weekend, or holiday — and we'll get a crew moving toward you. After a big storm we run flat out, and we get to people as fast as we safely can.
How fast can you get to me?
We're based in Harlingen with crews across the Valley, so we move quickly — often the same hour for a true emergency near our home base. After a major storm there's a lot of demand, so the most dangerous situations come first. Call us and we'll give you a straight answer on timing.
A tree is on a power line — what should I do?
Stay well clear and treat the line as live. Keep everyone back, don't touch the tree or anything touching it, and call your electric utility first to de-energize the line. Once it's safe, call us and we'll clear the tree. Your safety comes before the cleanup — always.
Do you help with insurance claims?
We can help on our end. If you're filing a claim, just let us know and we'll photograph and document the damage before we clear it, and give you an itemized estimate for your records. We're not insurance agents, but we'll make sure you have what you need from the tree side of things.
Does emergency service cost more after a storm?
Emergency work is priced for the size and difficulty of the job, not for how desperate you are. We don't gouge people after a storm. You'll always get a clear, upfront quote before we start, and the estimate is free — even at 2 a.m. in the middle of a hurricane cleanup.
Can you take care of the stump and the rest later?
Of course. The emergency is about making things safe fast, but we're a full tree service. Once the danger's handled, we can come back for stump grinding, finish any tree removal, or trim up what's left standing — all on the same straightforward terms.
