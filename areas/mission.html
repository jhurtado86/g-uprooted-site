Mission Is a Citrus Town — and We Keep Its Trees and Palms in Shape

Mission earned its "Home of the Grapefruit" name for a reason — the Ruby Red got its start here, and you still see citrus in backyards and along the edges of town. But Mission yards hold a lot more than fruit trees: live oaks, Texas ebony, anacua, and tall Mexican fan palms, especially through the established Sharyland neighborhoods. All of it needs the right hands to stay healthy and safe.

We're based in Harlingen, a straight run west on Expressway 83 and I-2, so getting a crew out to Mission is quick. You're calling a Valley team that already works the whole western side of the metro — from a backyard citrus and shade tree downtown to a full row of palms along a Sharyland boulevard.

From the Conway Avenue blocks downtown to the Cimarron golf neighborhoods and the groves out toward Bentsen State Park, we trim, remove, grind stumps, and clean up storm damage all across Mission. And the estimate is always free.
