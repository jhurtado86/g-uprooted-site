Salt Air and Wind Are Hard on Island Trees — Especially Palms

South Padre Island is a thin barrier island between the Laguna Madre and the open Gulf, and that setting changes everything about how trees grow here. Salt spray, sandy soil, and wind that never really stops mean the island's palms and the few hardy trees that take hold need more attention than they would inland. Brown, dead fronds, weak crowns, and seed pods hanging over walkways are common — and on a property full of guests, they're a safety issue, not just a look.

We're based in Harlingen, a straight run east on Highway 100 and over the Queen Isabella Causeway, so getting a crew onto the island is quick. Palm trimming and removal is our specialty, which makes South Padre a natural fit — we skin and shape Mexican fan and sabal palms, clear dead fronds, take down whatever the salt has killed, and haul it all off.

Most of our island work is for resorts, condos, RV parks, and vacation rentals along Padre Boulevard — places that need their landscaping sharp for guests and storm-ready when the season turns. And the estimate is always free.

Tree Service on South Padre Island — Common Questions

Do you offer same-day service on South Padre Island?
Often, yes. The island is a straight run east on Highway 100 and over the Queen Isabella Causeway from our Harlingen base, so we can usually get a crew out the same day or the next. Call us with the details and we'll tell you the soonest we can be there. Storm emergencies are handled 24/7.
Why do palms on the island need special care?
Salt and wind. Out on the island, palms drop dead fronds faster, build up seed pods that hang over walkways, and take a beating from steady Gulf wind. Left alone, that's both an eyesore and a hazard around guests and parked cars. Regular skinning and frond clearing keeps them clean and safe — and a well-kept palm handles a storm far better than a neglected one.
Do you work with resorts, RV parks, and rental properties?
Yes — that's most of our island work. We handle scheduled commercial tree and palm care for South Padre hotels, condos, RV parks, and vacation rentals. We can set up a regular trim cycle and work around your guest seasons, so the property always looks sharp and stays storm-ready.
What grows on the island, and what do you service?
The island is mostly palms — Mexican fan and sabal — along with a handful of salt-tolerant trees and shrubs that can take the conditions. We trim, skin, shape, and remove all of them, and clear out storm debris. Palms are our specialty, so the island's main job is right in our wheelhouse.
Are estimates really free?
Always. We'll look at the job, talk you through what it needs, and give you a clear, upfront quote at no charge — whether it's a single palm or a whole resort property. A couple of photos over the phone often get you a number even faster.
